PPC (Pay-Per-Click)

A digital marketing model where advertisers pay a fee each time one of their ads is clicked.

Description

Pay-Per-Click (PPC) is a model of internet marketing in which advertisers pay a fee each time one of their ads is clicked. Essentially, it is a way of buying visits to your site, rather than attempting to 'earn' those visits organically. PPC is commonly associated with search engines, where advertisers bid on keywords that they believe their target audiences will search for. When a user types in a query related to those keywords, the search engine displays ads alongside the organic results. The most common platform for PPC is Google Ads, where advertisers can create campaigns targeting specific keywords and demographics. This allows businesses to reach their audience more effectively and measure the success of their campaigns through various metrics such as click-through rates (CTR) and conversion rates. PPC can also be used on social media platforms, display networks, and more, making it a versatile tool in digital marketing strategies.

Examples

  • Google Ads: Advertisers bid on keywords to display ads in search results.
  • Facebook Ads: Businesses target specific demographics and interests to promote products.

Additional Information

  • PPC can provide immediate traffic to your website.
  • Effective keyword research is crucial for successful PPC campaigns.
