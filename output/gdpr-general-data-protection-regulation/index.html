GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation)

A comprehensive data protection law in the EU that regulates how personal data is processed and protected.

Description

The General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) is a sweeping data protection law that came into effect on May 25, 2018, in the European Union. It aims to enhance individuals' control and rights over their personal data while simplifying the regulatory environment for international business by unifying regulations across the EU. The GDPR mandates that organizations must obtain clear consent from individuals before collecting or processing their personal data, which includes information that can identify a person, such as names, emails, and IP addresses. It also gives individuals the right to access their data, request corrections, and demand deletion under certain circumstances. Businesses that fail to comply with GDPR may face significant fines, up to €20 million or 4% of annual global turnover, whichever is higher. The regulation emphasizes transparency and accountability, requiring businesses to implement appropriate security measures and notify authorities of data breaches within 72 hours. Overall, the GDPR represents a significant step toward enhancing privacy rights in the digital age.

Examples

  • A company must obtain explicit consent from users before sending them marketing emails.
  • Individuals have the right to request copies of their personal data held by a company.

Additional Information

  • GDPR applies to all organizations that process personal data of EU residents, regardless of where the organization is based.
  • The regulation has influenced data protection laws in other countries, leading to a global shift towards stricter privacy regulations.
