PA

PA stands for Page Authority.

Description

Page Authority (PA) is a search engine ranking score developed by Moz that predicts how well a specific page will rank on search engine result pages (SERPs). The score ranges from 1 to 100, with higher scores indicating a greater likelihood of ranking well. PA is calculated using multiple factors, including the number and quality of backlinks to the page, the relevance of the content, and various other on-page SEO elements. Page Authority is particularly useful for comparing the ranking potential of different pages, both within your own site and against competitors. While PA is not a direct ranking factor used by search engines like Google, it serves as a valuable metric for digital marketers and SEO professionals to gauge the strength of a page in the context of its ability to attract organic traffic. Improving PA can involve strategies such as optimizing content, increasing the quality and quantity of backlinks, and enhancing user experience on the page.

Examples

  • A blog post with high-quality backlinks from authoritative sites may have a PA of 50.
  • A product page with few backlinks and lower content quality might have a PA of 20.

Additional Information

  • PA is often used alongside Domain Authority (DA), which measures the overall strength of a website.
  • Tools like Moz, Ahrefs, and SEMrush provide metrics for PA as part of their SEO analytics.
