Ad Network

A platform that connects advertisers to websites that want to host advertisements.

Description

An Ad Network serves as an intermediary between advertisers and publishers, facilitating the buying and selling of advertising space. Advertisers use ad networks to promote their products or services across a variety of websites, apps, or other digital platforms. The ad network aggregates ad inventory from multiple publishers and offers it to advertisers, enabling them to reach a broader audience. This aggregation allows for better targeting and more efficient ad placement, as advertisers can select specific demographics, interests, or behaviors to reach their intended audience. Additionally, ad networks provide reporting and analytics to both advertisers and publishers, helping them understand the effectiveness of their campaigns. Some ad networks specialize in specific types of ads, such as display ads, video ads, or mobile ads. Overall, ad networks play a crucial role in the digital advertising ecosystem by streamlining the process of ad placement and enhancing the potential for monetization for publishers.

Examples

  • Google AdSense
  • Facebook Audience Network

Additional Information

  • Ad networks can be categorized into two types: vertical and horizontal.
  • Advertisers can use programmatic buying through ad networks for automated ad purchasing.
