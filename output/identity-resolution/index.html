Identity Resolution

The process of determining and linking multiple identifiers to a single individual across various platforms and devices.

Description

Identity Resolution is a critical practice in digital marketing that involves consolidating various identifiers (such as email addresses, device IDs, and cookies) associated with a consumer into a single, cohesive profile. This process helps marketers understand and engage with their audience more effectively by ensuring that they can track user behavior across different channels. For instance, a consumer may interact with a brand through various touchpoints, such as a website, a mobile app, and social media. Identity Resolution allows marketers to recognize that these interactions belong to the same person, enabling personalized marketing efforts. The process typically involves data collection, matching algorithms, and privacy considerations to ensure compliance with regulations like GDPR. By achieving a unified view of the consumer, brands can enhance their customer experiences, improve targeting accuracy, and drive better ROI on marketing campaigns.

Examples

  • Linking a customer's email subscription with their purchase history.
  • Identifying the same user across web, mobile, and in-store interactions.

Additional Information

  • Identity Resolution can help combat data silos within organizations.
  • Privacy and consent are key considerations in the identity resolution process.
