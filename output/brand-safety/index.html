Brand Safety

Measures taken to ensure that a brand's advertising does not appear in contexts that could harm its reputation.

Description

Brand safety refers to the practices and technologies that protect brands from being associated with negative or inappropriate content while advertising online. This includes ensuring that ads do not appear on websites or platforms that feature hate speech, violence, adult content, or any other material that could damage a brand's reputation. In the digital advertising landscape, where programmatic buying often places ads automatically, brand safety has become increasingly important. Advertisers use various tools and strategies, such as whitelists and blacklists, to curate where their ads are displayed. Additionally, brand safety is closely linked to consumer trust; brands that are found alongside harmful or controversial content risk losing customer loyalty and facing backlash. As a result, many brands now prioritize brand safety as a crucial component of their digital marketing strategies, investing in technologies that help ensure their advertisements are placed in safe environments.

Examples

  • A luxury fashion brand avoids advertising on websites that promote hate speech.
  • A family-friendly movie studio ensures its trailers do not run alongside violent content.

Additional Information

  • Brand safety is often managed through partnerships with ad verification companies.
  • The rise of social media has increased the complexity of maintaining brand safety.
