Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)

The total cost associated with acquiring a new customer.

Description

Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) is a critical metric in digital marketing and business strategy that quantifies the total expenses incurred to attract and convert a new customer. This cost includes various components such as marketing expenses, sales team salaries, advertising costs, and any promotional activities aimed at driving customer engagement. To calculate CAC, businesses typically sum all the costs related to acquiring customers over a specific period and divide that by the number of new customers gained in that same period. Understanding CAC is essential for businesses as it helps assess the efficiency of marketing strategies and the overall return on investment (ROI). A lower CAC indicates a more efficient acquisition strategy, while a higher CAC may signal the need for reevaluation of marketing efforts. Businesses often aim to optimize CAC to ensure that their lifetime value (LTV) of customers exceeds the cost of acquisition, leading to sustainable growth and profitability.

Examples

  • If a company spends $10,000 on marketing and gains 100 new customers, the CAC is $100.
  • A SaaS company spends $50,000 on digital ads and achieves 200 new subscriptions, resulting in a CAC of $250.

Additional Information

  • CAC is often compared with Customer Lifetime Value (CLV) to evaluate profitability.
  • Reducing CAC can involve improving marketing strategies, enhancing sales processes, and optimizing customer engagement.
