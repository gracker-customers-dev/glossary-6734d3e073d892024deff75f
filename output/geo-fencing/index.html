Geo-Fencing

A location-based service that uses GPS or RFID to create a virtual geographic boundary.

Description

Geo-fencing is a digital marketing strategy that utilizes GPS or RFID technology to set up a virtual boundary around a specific geographic area. When a device, such as a smartphone, enters or exits this area, it triggers a predefined action, like sending notifications, advertisements, or promotions to the user. This technology is particularly effective in targeting customers within a certain radius, allowing businesses to engage with potential customers in real time based on their location. For example, a retail store can send special offers to customers who walk within a certain distance of its location, encouraging them to visit the store. Geo-fencing also allows businesses to gather valuable data about customer behavior and preferences based on their movements and interactions within the defined area. Overall, geo-fencing enhances marketing strategies by providing personalized experiences and improving customer engagement through location-based targeting.

Examples

  • A coffee shop sends a discount coupon to customers when they enter a 500-meter radius.
  • A car dealership notifies potential buyers about a sale when they drive by the dealership.

Additional Information

  • Geo-fencing can be used in both B2C and B2B marketing strategies.
  • It requires user consent to access location data, which is crucial for privacy compliance.
