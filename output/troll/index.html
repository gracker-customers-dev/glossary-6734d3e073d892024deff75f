Troll

An individual who provokes others online by posting inflammatory or off-topic messages.

Description

In the context of digital marketing and online interactions, a 'troll' refers to someone who deliberately stirs up trouble by posting provocative, irrelevant, or offensive comments on social media platforms, forums, or other online spaces. Trolls often seek to elicit reactions from other users, creating chaos and distracting from the main topic of discussion. Their motivations can vary; some may do it for amusement, while others may have a more malicious intent, such as damaging a brand's reputation or disrupting a community. Trolls can be particularly harmful in digital marketing contexts, where they can undermine brand messaging and create negative sentiment around a product or service. Effective strategies to deal with trolls include ignoring them, moderating comments, and maintaining a strong and positive online presence to counteract the negativity they bring.

Examples

  • A user posts a derogatory comment on a brand's promotional post to provoke outrage.
  • An individual shares a misleading claim about a competitor's product in an online forum.

Additional Information

  • Trolling can lead to significant backlash if not addressed properly by brands.
  • Many social media platforms have developed tools to report and manage trolls.
